Academic Rigor to Industrial Impact

I hold a PhD in Mathematics from IIT Bombay with postdoctoral experience at IIT Madras. My work has evolved from theoretical research in manifold theory to building practical models for the aerospace industry. Currently, I focus on log data analysis and mathematical modeling to improve UAV technologies.

Exploring the industrial landscape after years in academia is a rewarding shift. I am interested in how deep mathematical rigor can be applied to fast-paced sectors like aviation and autonomous systems. I look forward to using my analytical background to solve practical, high-impact problems outside of a traditional research setting.

Navigating the Transition

Moving from a focused academic background into an industrial role is less about leaving your training behind and more about finding a new way to apply it. The core strength of a mathematician or physicist—the analytical thinking and mental rigor—is exactly what is needed to solve complex problems in technology.

I’ve put together this collection as a potential starting point for that journey. While no single list can cover every career path, this serves as a possible direction ahead for those looking to bridge the gap.

For Maths & Stats: If you are comfortable with proofs but new to hardware, start with Section IV (Mechanics).
For Physics: If you have the intuition but lack coding, focus on Section V (The Toolkit).
For Everyone: Section II (Data Science & ML) offers a look at how probability drives modern systems.
